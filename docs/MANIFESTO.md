Everyone else guesses.

## The Hard Questions

### "Why not just ask ChatGPT?"

ChatGPT doesn't know what it doesn't know about Rwanda. It will confidently name fintech companies that don't exist or miss half the ones that do. It has no access to RDB's current company registry, BNR's latest licensing framework, or RRA's current tax filing deadlines. Most critically, it cannot tell you what *your* company specifically needs to do — because it has no knowledge of your business, your sector, your registration status, or your compliance history.

We are different in three specific ways:
1. **Grounded in verified Rwanda data** — every answer is drawn from a curated knowledge base, not the internet at large
2. **Cited** — every claim points to a specific regulation, document, or data source
3. **Personalized** — the compliance engine knows your business specifically and generates answers tailored to your exact situation

### "Why not hire a consultant?"

You should hire a consultant when you need strategic judgment on a complex, high-stakes decision. You shouldn't need a consultant to find out which companies are in a sector, what the basic compliance requirements are, or how Rwanda's PSP licensing process works. Consultants charge $200–$500/hour. We charge a monthly subscription. More importantly: we're available at 2am when you're filling in a form, we don't give you a generic answer because your case seems standard, and we don't have a conflict of interest.

We are not replacing consultants for complex advisory work. We are eliminating the need to hire a consultant for information retrieval and basic compliance navigation.

### "What happens when the AI lies to me?"

This is the right question to ask. Here's how we prevent it:

1. **RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation):** The AI only answers from our curated knowledge base, not from its general training data. If the answer isn't in our knowledge base, it says "I don't have verified data on this."
2. **Citations:** Every AI response includes a source reference. You can verify the claim yourself.
3. **Confidence scoring:** The system flags when it's operating near the boundary of its knowledge.
4. **Human review layer:** All regulatory information goes through a verification process before entering the knowledge base.
5. **"Not sure" is an option:** We train and prompt the AI to prefer saying "I don't have enough verified information to answer this" over inventing an answer.

The AI is not infallible. We make that clear. We are a decision-support tool, not a licensed legal advisor.

### "What about my privacy? I'm uploading sensitive business documents."

Your documents are your documents. They live in an encrypted, isolated vault tied to your organization account. We do not train any AI models on your uploaded documents. We do not share your data with other users or organizations. We do not use your business information for market research or data products.

The compliance engine reads your documents only to provide you with compliance guidance. That context is ephemeral and private.

We follow GDPR-inspired data protection principles as our standard, regardless of the current state of Rwandan data protection law. We treat your data as if our own business depended on its security — because it does.

### "Where does your data come from?"

Three layers:

1. **Primary regulatory sources**: RDB, RRA, BNR, RURA, RSB, PSF, and the Official Gazette of Rwanda. We have ingestion pipelines for their public publications and, where available, API partnerships.
2. **Market intelligence**: Company websites, LinkedIn, press releases, African startup databases, and curated news sources — processed through NLP pipelines and human verification.
3. **User contributions** (Phase 3): Verified companies can claim and update their own profiles, creating a Wikipedia-style layer of self-reported data — with editorial oversight.

We are transparent about data freshness. Every data point shows when it was last verified.

### "Can't you just do this without AI?"

Parts of it, yes. The company directory is a database problem, not an AI problem. The static compliance checklists are a content problem.

But here's where AI is irreplaceable: when you ask "what do I need to do to register a fintech company that does cross-border mobile payments targeted at SMEs?" — that question has no static answer. It depends on your transaction volume, your banking partners, your target markets, and a dozen other variables that branch into different regulatory paths. The AI synthesizes across those branches in real time, in plain language, specific to you.

AI also allows the system to improve as regulations change. New regulation published in the Official Gazette → ingested → embedded → instantly reflected in compliance guidance. Without AI, you'd need to manually update every affected compliance checklist.

### "What are you doing? Explain in one sentence."

We are organizing Rwanda's business and regulatory information into a searchable, AI-powered platform so that any investor, founder, or researcher can find what they need in seconds instead of weeks.
